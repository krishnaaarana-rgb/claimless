## What Claimless Does

Claimless is the verification layer between "candidate applies" and "candidate reaches your desk."

Instead of trusting what someone *claims* they can do, we analyse what they've *actually built* — then we interview them with an AI that's smarter than any phone screener you've ever hired.

### Stage 1: Proof of Work Verification

When a candidate enters your pipeline, Claimless automatically:

- **Scrapes and analyses their GitHub repositories** — not just "do they have a GitHub?" but what did they actually build? What architectural decisions did they make? Do they write tests? How do they handle complexity?
- **Analyses shipped products and portfolio work** — real things in production, not side projects that never launched
- **Cross-references everything against their resume** — if they say "Led a team of 8 on a microservices migration" but their GitHub shows solo frontend work, we flag it

The output: a **verified candidate profile** with skill scores backed by evidence, not self-assessment. Every score links back to the actual work that proves it.

*Your recruiters get a candidate brief that's more accurate than 3 rounds of interviews could produce — before they've spent a single minute on the phone.*

### Stage 2: AI Voice Interview

This is where it gets powerful.

Claimless conducts a live voice interview with every candidate. But this isn't your typical chatbot asking scripted questions. This is an AI interviewer that:

- **Already knows the candidate's verified work** — it's read their code, analysed their projects, cross-referenced their resume. It walks in more prepared than any human interviewer.
- **Adapts in real-time** — if a candidate says "I scaled a system to handle 10x traffic," our AI doesn't just nod. It gives them a live scenario: *"You just got paged — cache is stampeding, thousands of requests hitting expired keys. Walk me through what you do right now."* Then adds complications. Then goes deeper.
- **Goes 3 levels deep on every claim** — "How did you measure that? What was the first bottleneck? After you fixed that, what broke next?" If they can answer all three with specifics, they did the work. If they get vague at level 2, they watched someone else do it.
- **Detects coached candidates** — many candidates now prep with AI tools. They give perfect STAR-format answers but crumble on unexpected follow-ups. Our interviewer breaks rehearsed patterns: "Great example — now tell me about a time the OPPOSITE happened."
- **Speaks like a human** — warm, conversational, uses filler words. Candidates don't feel like they're talking to a robot. They feel like they're talking to a sharp, well-prepared hiring manager.

The output: a **scored interview assessment** with evidence from the transcript, skill-by-skill evaluation, red flags, green flags, and a hire/no-hire recommendation.

*Your recruiters get a complete candidate evaluation — proof of work + live interview performance — before they pick up the phone. When they DO call, they already know this person is worth their time.*
